Ted Neward - Rethinking Enterprise


Updating Brooks' "No Silver Bullet" with "No Best Practices"  
Trying to breathe underwater is a bad idea.  It sounds like common sense.  
Until you realize he's espousing critical thinking.  The opposite of common sense.
I'm not going to tell you what to do, to use the same old box arrow box arrow cylinder
I'm going to ask / empower you to to go and reason through what's appropriate for your situation.
He exhorts us to resist the temptation of the familiar, the lure of the cognitively proximal
But just beginning a project, selecting a framework is computationally infeasible
When taking time to dig past platitudes into the actual pros and cons of each.
The complexity of the problem space puts it beyond the ability to grok.
Flat-landers in N-dimensional, non-euclidean enterprise space
But some of us gutter-bound are looking at the stars, 
Both the beauty and the cosmic horrors.


It’s always “rethinking”
You can never safely step back and say:
 “O.K.  I just rethought my enterprise.  That was hard, but I’m glad that’s over with”
Technologies come, change, and go.  Feeding the geek’s neophilic need.
Environments change and requirements are revealed.
Solving new problems is what keeps us going.


The dude of the software world
