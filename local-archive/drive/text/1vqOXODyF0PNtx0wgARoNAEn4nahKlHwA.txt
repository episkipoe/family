Phase 1: Catching the Cicadas
It was a relatively cool day, heavy cloud cover; they weren't as active as they usually are. 
I plucked them off of branches and put them in the potato salad container.
Easy to catch, kinda creepy when they grab ya.
This might be the first time I met my food while it was still alive.
I mean I've seen cows, pigs, goats, etc. But never the same ones that I've eaten.
The Internet advised me to catch the females, there's more tasty fat in them.
I'm not sure how to discern the sex of a cicada
Wound up with about half a cup.
I shake the container to stun them.Phase 2: Cooking
In the potato salad container they are huddled together.
Frantic or frolicking; I can't quite tell.
I begin to single them out and decapitate them.
They seem to take this in blasé stride.
I mean, they aren't really less active with their heads disconnected
Yellow gunk kinda oozes out of them.
It looks pretty gross.
Most recipes recommend removing head, legs, wings.
But one site said it was okay to eat them. So I do.
Dump the writhing mass into the hot oil.
There is a rather audible reaction.
And soon they become just food.Phase 3: Eating
The curry is flavorful, but not all that spicy.
The cicadas crunch as you bite them
a rather pleasant experience
I can't really taste them distinctly
(that's the reason the experiment took this direction)
the theory is: if you can curry it I can eat it.
