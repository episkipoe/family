Urban Chestnut
Dark clouds overhead.
Thunder in the distance.
It's warm.  I'm in t-shirt and shorts.
The blowing wind smells of life and a strong cup of coffee.
  





I order Thrale's.  A Russian Imperial Stout.
She pours me a pint of it, bless her heart.
She must be new.
Blue towel tucked into the back pocket of her jeans.
A more senior barman pulls her aside. 
For a sidebar, in stage whisper, in earshot.
That's the wrong glass.  It should be in a snifter.
"It's 9% alcohol.  That's like 2, it's literally 2 of them"
I mean, it is three squirrels worth of IBU and ABV
She comes over to relay the news to me.
Thank you.


It's a nice, solid entry in the category. 
Not choking and bitter, but rather seems somewhat weak.  4.5


Next I ask for something stout. 
O'Florians, an Irish, "like Guinnness"
Not bad, but tastes too sweet, too much like a brown.  3.5


I overhear them talking about another offering, Erlkönig
There's debate on how to pronounce it correctly.
"It translates to 'Elf King' which is way cooler, so I'd just go with 'Elf King'"
The Elf King has fallen.  The spigot spouts foam.


This guy here is twice given props on his Bluth's Frozen Banana shirt.
Ordering growlers for his friend, his house was hit by a tornado.


I'm taking notes on a sheet of paper and I see this guy consulting his phone, looking for a new data point.
Now entering his selection.
What's that?
He's using the Ratebeer App



A financial analysis geek who's here with his lawyer, in real Fear and Loathing style.
They're beer nerds too, fascinated by the bits of analysis I've printed out.
Making suggestions on additions to my spreadsheet.
Sight, smell, taste, price, accessibility.
Recommending breweries that I add to my list.
Kirkwood, Ferguson, Perennial, Square One, Morgan St, etc.
We're drawing box&whisker plots, conflicting Gaussian distributions.
They bought me two rounds.  A fantastic flippin' time. 
And I take home with a growler of Elf King.
